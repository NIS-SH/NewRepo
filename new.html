Classic Omelette

Serves: 1 • Prep: 5 mins • Cook: 5 mins

Ingredients

  • 2–3 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp milk or water (optional, for fluffiness)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp butter or oil
  • Fillings (optional): shredded cheese, chopped herbs, diced tomatoes, onions, mushrooms, or ham

Steps

  1. Crack the eggs into a bowl. Add milk or water if using. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  2. Whisk briskly until the yolks and whites are fully combined and slightly frothy.
  3. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat and add the butter. Swirl to coat the pan.
  4. Pour the beaten eggs into the pan. Let them set for a few seconds, then gently push the cooked edges toward the center so uncooked egg flows to the edges.
  5. When the top is mostly set but still slightly wet, add your fillings to one half of the omelette.
  6. Fold the other half over the fillings using a spatula and cook 20–30 seconds more until cheese melts and eggs are cooked through but tender.
  7. Slide onto a plate and serve immediately. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.

Tips: Use moderate heat to avoid browning. For a fluffy omelette, whisk more air into the eggs and avoid overcooking.
